PSVM 1

Invoice

A compact invoice-calculation VM with explicit business ops, exact money arithmetic, worker execution, and a shipped next-op student bundle.

Ops

  • READ_ITEM
  • LINE_TOTAL
  • ADD_SUBTOTAL
  • APPLY_TAX
  • EMIT_TOTAL
  • HALT

What it proves

  • Minimum business-logic ISA can stay small and exact.
  • Worker execution is enough for a usable browser app loop.
  • The runtime is legible enough to become model supervision later.

PSVM 2

Sudoku 4x4

A task-shaped Sudoku VM with canonical search actions, streamed from a worker so the full solve stays inspectable from focus to halt.

Ops

  • FOCUS_NEXT
  • READ_CANDS
  • PLACE
  • UNDO
  • FAIL
  • HALT

What it proves

  • The VM surface can follow search semantics instead of generic bytecode.
  • Canonical traces make later transformer training realistic.
  • 4x4 is small enough to debug ISA and trace issues before 9x9.
Open Sudoku PSVM

Why PSVM first

The landing page now shows only the problem-shaped VM track. That keeps the repo centered on the smallest sound ISA, canonical traces, and exact worker runtimes.

  • Domain ops are easier to verify than generic execution tokens.
  • Short exact traces are better training targets than one-shot answers.
  • The same shape can grow into model-backed executors later.

Routes kept separate

The older transformer-policy and 9x9 WASM pages can still exist as direct routes, but they are no longer the default story at `/`.

  • Landing page scope stays aligned with the PSVM direction.
  • Embeds can point straight at `/invoice/` or `/soduku/`.
  • The repo can still keep experimental routes without promoting them.

Default landing routes: /invoice/ and /soduku/.
