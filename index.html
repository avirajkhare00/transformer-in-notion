      Why these demos
      
          
      
            The early gallery favors exact, visual problems with short state
            representations. They are easy to verify, small enough for local
            weights, and easy to explain as prompt, program, trace, and result.
          
      
          
        
            
      • Tic-tac-toe gives instant interaction and obvious correctness.
        • 
            
      • Sudoku gives a longer, more mechanical WASM execution trace.
        • 
            
      • Next demos like 24 Game, sorting, or maze search keep the same shape.
        • 
          
      
        
      

        
      
          
      Why not yet
      
          
      
            Bigger examples are deferred until the local model and executor path
            is more mature.
          
      
          
        
            
      • Full chess needs stricter move legality and much stronger search.
        • 
            
      • Large puzzles increase model size, cold start, and browser cost.
        • 
            
      • Weak results break trust faster on complex games than on exact mini demos.
        • 
          
      
        
      

        
      
          
      New PSVM
      
          
      
            The new Weiqi prototype keeps the board small and the op set tight:
            exact 5x5 capture problems with worker-side search and a visible
            move/capture/undo trace.
          
      
          
      
            
              Open Weiqi PSVM
            
          
      
        
      
      
      

      
      
        
      
          Tic-tac-toe now runs from local model weights in the embed. Sudoku now
          runs from a browser-side WASM executor. The PSVM track now also
          includes a small-board Weiqi capture prototype. All of them share the
          same prompt, program, trace, and result contract.
